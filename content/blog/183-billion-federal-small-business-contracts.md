$183 billion in prime contracts went to small businesses in FY2024.

That's 28.8% of all federal contracting dollars. Fourth consecutive year of growth.

And yet, most small business owners say the same thing:

> "I didn't even know I could bid on government contracts."

## The Government Is Required to Award You Business

The government is legally required to award at least 23% of contracts to small businesses. They're exceeding that. Agencies that fall short face scrutiny from the SBA and Congress.

On top of that, there are set-aside programs that reserve contracts exclusively for specific groups:

- **8(a) businesses** — $15.4B in FY2024
- **Service-Disabled Veteran-Owned (SDVOSB)** — $8.5B
- **Women-Owned Small Business (WOSB)** — $31.7B
- **HUBZone** — $17.4B

If you hold one of these certifications, there are contracts set aside specifically for you. The opportunity is real.

## The Hardest Part Isn't Qualifying

The hardest part isn't qualifying. It's finding the right opportunity before the deadline passes.

SAM.gov posts thousands of opportunities every week. Most contractors don't have a system to filter the noise. They either miss relevant contracts entirely or spend hours manually searching through irrelevant results.
